Practice Exercise: Network Inspection Checklist

Objective: Conduct a thorough physical inspection of a configured network to ensure readiness and identify potential issues.

Scenario:

You are a network technician tasked with inspecting a newly configured office network before it goes live. Use the checklist below to verify all components.


Step 1: Cable Connections

Locate all network cables (Ethernet, power, etc.).

Check for:
  • Secure connections (no loose plugs).
  • Signs of damage (frayed wires, bent pins).
  • Correct port assignments (e.g., WAN port for modem, LAN ports for devices).

    • Task: List any issues found (e.g., "Ethernet cable near Router A is unplugged").


    Step 2: Device Inspection

    Examine the following devices:
  • Router
  • Switch
  • Wireless Access Point (WAP)
  • Modem

    • Check for:
  • Physical damage (cracks, broken antennas).
  • Status lights (e.g., power, Wi-Fi, LAN activity). Are any blinking red or off?

    • Task: Note any abnormal lights or damage (e.g., "Router power light is off").


    Step 3: Labeling & Organization

    Verify:
  • Are cables labeled (e.g., "Router to Switch")?
  • Are cables neatly organized (not tangled)?
  • Are devices placed securely (not stacked or overheating)?

    • Task: Suggest improvements (e.g., "Label the cable connecting Switch Port 3 to WAP").


    Step 4: Simulated Problem-Solving

    Imagine this issue: A user reports "no internet" on their computer.

    During inspection, you find:
  • The Ethernet cable is plugged into the router’s WAN port (should be LAN).
  • The router’s power cable is loose.

    • Task:
  • How would you fix these issues?
  • Why does plugging into the WAN port cause problems?
