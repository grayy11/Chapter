STEP BY STEP PROCEDURE

Step-by-step Procedure When Installing Network Cables and Cable Raceways

  • Assess Your Needs: The first major step in the planning process is to establish the actual number of devices that need to be connected to the network, such as computers, printers, servers, and other network devices. This will help determine the number of network ports necessary and the type of cable to use.

  • Create a Layout: Sketch a detailed layout of the area where you intend to install the cables. Draw the placement of network devices and the path for the wires. Locate your primary node (such as a network switch or router), which is typically in a server room or network closet.

  • Measure Cable Lengths: Calculate the cable lengths needed for future devices by measuring distances from the central hub and summing up the lengths. Add extra length to accommodate bends around barriers and conduits.

  • Check Building Codes: Ensure compliance with local construction and installation requirements, especially regarding cabling systems. This may include fire safety measures and regulations for running wires through walls or ceilings.

  • Assess the Installation Environment: Evaluate factors such as the layout of the space, existing infrastructure, obstacles like walls or ceilings, electrical interference, and compliance with building codes. This ensures proper planning and execution of cable routes to optimize network performance.

  • Preparing for Installation: Planning helps ensure successful installation. Consider the following steps:

  • A strong network starts with proper cable installation! This process walks you through planning routes, selecting materials, cutting and connecting cables, and ensuring compliance for a seamless, interference-free setup. Master these steps to build reliable networks for any environment!

  • Gathering Necessary Tools and Materials: Consolidate all essential tools and materials for the upcoming installation. These may include:


    • - RJ45 connectors
    - Cable Tester
    - Cable stripper and cutter
    - Crimping tool
    - Fish tape or cable puller
    - Cable ties and clips
    - Wall plates and faceplates
    - Drill and drill bits
    - Screwdriver
    - Label maker or masking tape and marker
    - Network cables (Cat5e, Cat6, Cat6a, or Cat7)


  • Planning Cable Routes and Paths: Map out the paths for network cables, considering distance, obstacles, and cable management. Identify connected devices, define access points, and plan routes through walls, ceilings, or floors. Key factors include avoiding barriers like furniture and electrical wiring, minimizing interference, and ensuring easy maintenance access.

  • Measuring and Cutting Cables to Length: When trimming network cables to the appropriate length, measure the space between networked devices, and include extra length for organizing the cable inside the device link. Use specialized cutters or cable scissors to ensure a clean cut.

  • Stripping the Cable Jackets: Stripping the jackets of network cables is done using a cable stripper. This removes the outer sheathing to reveal the wire pairs. This is an essential step before terminating or connecting cables to devices like connectors or wall plates.

Installing RJ45 Connectors

Installing RJ45 connectors involves the following steps:

  1. Assemble the cable and untwist the pairs according to T568A or T568B standards.
  2. Insert the wires into the RJ45 connector, ensuring the correct orientation is followed.
  3. Use a crimping tool to secure the wires in place, creating a functional Ethernet cable connection.

Connecting the Cable to Your Devices

Note the ports on the switches, routers, or computers to which the cables should be connected. Tighten and reconnect each wire to ensure a firm and proper connection. Follow the manufacturer’s instructions for setting IP addresses, subnet masks, and other network parameters.
